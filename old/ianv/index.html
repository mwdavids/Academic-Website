About Me

I am a PhD student in the Political Science Department at the University of California, San Diego. The overarching research question I explore is: "How do interpersonal relationships affect behavior and identity?" This question has served as a lens through which I have sought to understand problems related to governance, inter-personal and group conflict. I am a co-organizer of UCSD's Human Nature Group.

I speak fluent Tagalog (Filipino) and have spent more than more than two years living and conducting research in the Philippines. I have also done field work in India and Thailand. For detailed information about my academic experience and research, please see my CV.

Data and Code

I work primarily in R, and have made public some code that might be helpful for others:
  1. Multidimensional Measures of Social Structure. These functions will calculate fractionalization, bipolarization, cross-cuttingness measures from survey or census data. [Under development][Paper by Joel S. Selway]
  2. Proportional Representation Seat Calculator. This code calculates the number of seats different parties should be awarded given the number of votes they have received.
